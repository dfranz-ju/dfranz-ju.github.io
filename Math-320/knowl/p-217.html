In some sense, this chapter is the culmination of our studies in Linear Algebra so far. The study of eigenvalues and eigenvectors relies on knowledge of linear transformations, the determinant, solving for a null space, and bases. "Eigenstuff", as material related to eigenvalues and eigenvectors is often called, is one of the most widely applicable areas of Linear Algebra. They are used in economics, genetics ("eigengenes"), physics (eigenfunctions), dynamical systems, data science, statistics, and many more areas. We will merely touch the surface by building the mathematical foundation upon which all these applications rest.
