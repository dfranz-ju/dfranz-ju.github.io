One of the main reasons to find or create a basis for a vector space is so that we can impose a coordinate system on that vector space. We already have an understanding of coordinate systems from years of experience with the \(xy\) plane, where we represent points with their \(x\) and \(y\) coordinates. This is also how we have been writing column vectors in \(\mathbb{R}^n\text{.}\) This entire time, we have been using coordinates with respect to the standard basis of \(\mathbb{R}^n\text{.}\)
