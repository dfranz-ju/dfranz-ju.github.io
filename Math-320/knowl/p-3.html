As an example of a Babylonian problem, consider the following, which has been slightly modified from the original found on a clay tablet from about 300 BCE: There are two fields whose total area is 1800 square yards. One produces grain at a rate of \(\frac{2}{3}\) bushel per square yard, the other at a rate of \(\frac{1}{2}\) bushel per square yard. The total yield of the two fields is 1100 bushels. What is the size of each field?
