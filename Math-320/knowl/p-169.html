This chapter takes that observation and runs with it. The first section starts by defining a vector not as a specific type of object, but as a kind of object that satisfies the usual properties of addition and scalar multiplication. We then see several examples of spaces with objects that satisfy these properties and call them "vector spaces." Throughout the chapter we will see that most of the work we have done so far with \(\mathbb{R}^n\) vectors can also be applied to other vector spaces.
