You should be able to perform these tasks with reasonable fluency when you arrive to class.

  • Given a basis \(\mathcal{B}\) and the \(\mathcal{B}\)-coordinate vector of \(\mathbf{x}\text{,}\) express \(\mathbf{x}\) with respect to the standard basis.

  • Given a basis \(\mathcal{B}\) and a vector \(\mathbf{x}\text{,}\) find the \(\mathcal{B}\)-coordinate vector of \(\mathbf{x}\text{.}\)

  • Find the change-of-coordinates matrix of a given basis \(\mathcal{B}\) from \(\mathcal{B}\) to the standard basis.
