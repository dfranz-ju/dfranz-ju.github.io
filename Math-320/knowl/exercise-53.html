Exercise 1.

Let \(\mathbf{v}_1 = \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 2 \\ 3 \end{bmatrix}, \mathbf{v}_2 = \begin{bmatrix} 3 \\ 2 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix}, \mathbf{v}_3 = \begin{bmatrix} 2 \\ 3 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix}.\) Then \(\mathcal{B}=\{\mathbf{v}_1, \mathbf{v}_2, \mathbf{v}_3\}\) if a basis for \(\mathbb{R}^3\) (check this).

  1. Suppose the \(\mathcal{B}\)-coordinate vector of \(\mathbf{x}\) is \(\begin{bmatrix} 2 \\ -3 \\ 4 \end{bmatrix}\text{.}\) Express \(\mathbf{x}\) with respect to the standard basis (i.e. the normal way).

  2. Let \(\mathbf{y}=12\mathbf{e}_1=\begin{bmatrix} 12 \\ 0 \\ 0 \end{bmatrix}\text{.}\) Find the \(\mathcal{B}\)-coordinate vector of \(\mathbf{y}.\)

  3. Find the change of coordinates matrix from \(\mathcal{B}\) to the standard basis.
