This last point, the coordinate mapping, is the focus of the end of the section. It allows us to use our \(\mathbb{R}^n\) knowledge and computational abilities on other vector spaces such as \(\mathbb{P}_n\text{.}\) In other words, it lets us translate problems in any (finite dimensional) vector space to problems in a specific \(\mathbb{R}^n\text{.}\) This is accomplished formally through the concept of isomorphisms.
