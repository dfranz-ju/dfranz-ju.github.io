Section 1.5 Guided Practice 1.5: Solution Sets of Linear Systems

Overview.

In the previous sections, we have studied systems of linear equations in many forms, using augmented matrices, vector equations, and matrix equations. In this section we delve deeper into how we describe the solution sets of linear systems, specifically in the case of infinitely many solutions (it is pretty easy to describe no solutions or 1 solution). This is accomplished using vector notation, which also opens up geometric descriptions of solution sets.

The section begins by examining homogeneous linear systems, lineasr systems whose matrix equations are of the form $A\mathbf{x}=\mathbf{0}$. These are easier to study and, surpisingly, an understanding of the solution sets of homogeneous systems is all we need to understand solution sets of all linear systems, as is made clear in Theorem 6.

Subsection 1.5.1 Basic Learning Objectives

You should be able to perform these tasks with reasonable fluency when you arrive to class.

  • Recognize when a linear system is homogeneous.
  • Determine when a homogeneous system has a nontrivial solution.

Subsection 1.5.2 Advanced Learning Objectives

In addition to mastering the basic objectives, here are the tasks you should be able to perform after class, with practice:

  • Describe the solution set of a homogeneous system as the span of a set of vectors.
  • Describe solutions to linear systems in parametric vector form.
  • Use Theorem 6 to geometrically compare and contrast the solution sets of \(A\mathbf{x}=\mathbf{b}\) and \(A\mathbf{x}=\mathbf{0}\text{.}\)

Subsection 1.5.3 Resources

Reading: Read Section 1.5 of the textbook, keeping the learning objectives in mind. It's helpful to write down new vocabulary words and notation along with short definitions/examples and to actively work along with the examples on your own paper.

The discussion on pages 46 and 47 explains the geometric interpretation of solution sets of related homogeneous and non-homogeneous linear systems.
