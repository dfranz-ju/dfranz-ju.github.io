Section 4.4 Guided Practice 4.4: Coordinate Systems

Overview.

One of the main reasons to find or create a basis for a vector space is so that we can impose a coordinate system on that vector space. We already have an understanding of coordinate systems from years of experience with the \(xy\) plane, where we represent points with their \(x\) and \(y\) coordinates. This is also how we have been writing column vectors in \(\mathbb{R}^n\text{.}\) This entire time, we have been using coordinates with respect to the standard basis of \(\mathbb{R}^n\text{.}\)

What this section explores is writing coordinates using other bases, in the case of \(\mathbb{R}^n\text{,}\) or just writing coordinates at all in the case of other vector spaces. Changing between coordinate systems can be implemented using a change-of-coordinates matrix; this kind of operation is very common in physics. But also, by imposing coordinates on vector spaces other than \(\mathbb{R}^n\text{,}\) we can treat them in the same way as we do \(\mathbb{R}^n.\)

This last point, the coordinate mapping, is the focus of the end of the section. It allows us to use our \(\mathbb{R}^n\) knowledge and computational abilities on other vector spaces such as \(\mathbb{P}_n\text{.}\) In other words, it lets us translate problems in any (finite dimensional) vector space to problems in a specific \(\mathbb{R}^n\text{.}\) This is accomplished formally through the concept of isomorphisms.

Subsection 4.4.1 Basic Learning Objectives

You should be able to perform these tasks with reasonable fluency when you arrive to class.

  • Given a basis \(\mathcal{B}\) and the \(\mathcal{B}\)-coordinate vector of \(\mathbf{x}\text{,}\) express \(\mathbf{x}\) with respect to the standard basis.

  • Given a basis \(\mathcal{B}\) and a vector \(\mathbf{x}\text{,}\) find the \(\mathcal{B}\)-coordinate vector of \(\mathbf{x}\text{.}\)

  • Find the change-of-coordinates matrix of a given basis \(\mathcal{B}\) from \(\mathcal{B}\) to the standard basis.

Subsection 4.4.2 Advanced Learning Objectives

In addition to mastering the basic objectives, here are the tasks you should be able to perform after class, with practice:

  • Interpret the coordinate mapping as a one-to-one and onto linear transformation.

  • Describe what an isomorphism of two vector spaces is.

  • Give examples of isomorphic vector spaces.

  • Use coordinate mappings to computationally answer questions about vector spaces other than \(\mathbb{R}^n.\)
