Section 5.2 Guided Practice 5.2: The Characteristic Polynomial

Overview.

In Section 5.1, we learned about eigenvalues and eigenvectors. The computational focus was on finding eigenvectors once we had an eigenvalue in hand. But how do we find eigenvalues in the first place? We return to the original definition and bring in the determinant to help out. The section begins by motivating the use of the determinant to find eigenvalues and then recalls the relevant properties of the determinant (a good refresher).

The text then defines the characteristic polynomial, which is central to the study of eigenvalues and eigenvectors. This polynomial is used to define the multiplicity of an eigenvalue and to prove an important result about similar matrices. The section closes with an application to dynamical systems; in class we will do a similar analysis applied to the Fibonacci sequence.

Subsection 5.2.1 Basic Learning Objectives

You should be able to perform these tasks with reasonable fluency when you arrive to class.

  • Use the determinant to find the characteristic polynomial of a square matrix.

  • Use the characteristic equation to find the eigenvalues of square matrix.

  • Find the (algebraic) multiplicities of the eigenvalues of a square matrix.

Subsection 5.2.2 Advanced Learning Objectives

In addition to mastering the basic objectives, here are the tasks you should be able to perform after class, with practice:

  • Describe what it means for two matrices to be similar.

  • Explain why similar matrices have the same eigenvalues.

1.

2.
