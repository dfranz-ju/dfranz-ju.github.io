Solution 2.6.7.1.

Use integration by parts with

\begin{align*} u \amp = \ln(t) \amp dv \amp = t^2 \, dt\\ du \amp = \frac{1}{t} \, dt \amp v \amp = \frac{t^3}{3} \end{align*}

Then

\begin{align*} \int t^2 \ln(t) \, dt \amp = \ln(t) \cdot \frac{t^3}{3} - \int \frac{t^3}{3} \cdot \frac{1}{t} \, dt\\ \amp = \frac{1}{3} t^3 \ln(t) - \int \frac{1}{3} t^2 \, dt\\ \amp = \frac{1}{3} t^3 \ln(t) - \frac{1}{3} \cdot \frac{t^3}{3} + C \end{align*}
