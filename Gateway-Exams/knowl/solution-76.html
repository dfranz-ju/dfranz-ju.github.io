Solution 2.2.4.1.

First decompose the integrand into partial fractions:

\begin{equation*} \frac{10}{(x-2)(x+5)} = \frac{A}{x-2} + \frac{B}{x+5} \end{equation*}

This yields

\begin{equation*} 10 = A(x+5) + B(x-2)\text{.} \end{equation*}

Plug in \(x=2\) to solve for \(A\) and \(x=-5\) to solve for \(B\text{:}\)

\begin{equation*} 10 = 7A + 0 \implies A=\frac{10}{7} \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} 10= 0 -7B \implies B=-\frac{10}{7} \end{equation*}

Now perform the integration:

\begin{align*} \int \frac{10}{(x-2)(x+5)} \, dx \amp = \int \left( \frac{10}{7}\frac{1}{x-2} - \frac{10}{7} \frac{1}{x+5} \right) \, dx\\ \amp = \frac{10}{7}\ln|x-2| - \frac{10}{7} \ln|x+5| + C \end{align*}
