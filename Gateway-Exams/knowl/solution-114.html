Solution 2.7.2.1.

Use integration by parts with

\begin{align*} u \amp = 2x \amp dv \amp = \cos(3x)\, dx\\ du \amp = 2 \, dx \amp v \amp = \dfrac{1}{3}\sin(3x) \end{align*}

Then

\begin{align*} \int 2x\cos(3x) \, dx \amp = 2x\cdot \frac{1}{3} \sin(3x) - \int 2 \cdot \frac{1}{3} \sin(3x) \, dx\\ \amp = \frac{2}{3}x\sin(3x) - \frac{2}{3} \cdot \frac{1}{3}\cdot -\cos(3x) + C\\ \amp = \frac{2}{3}x\sin(3x) +\frac{2}{9} \cos(3x) + C \end{align*}
