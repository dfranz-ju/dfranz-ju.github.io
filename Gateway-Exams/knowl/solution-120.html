Solution 2.7.8.1.

Use substitution with \(u=5w^2 + 1\) and \(du=10w \, dw\text{,}\) so \(\dfrac{1}{10} \, du = w \, dw\text{:}\)

\begin{align*} \int 3w\sin(w^2+1) \, dw \amp = \int 3 \cdot \frac{1}{10} \sin(u) \, du\\ \amp = -\frac{3}{10} \cos(u) + C\\ \amp = -\frac{3}{10} \cos(5w^2 + 1) + C \end{align*}
