Use substitution with \(u=\sin(2\theta)\) and \(du = 2\cos(2\theta) \, d\theta\text{,}\) so \(\dfrac{1}{2} \, du = \cos(2\theta) \, d\theta\text{:}\)

\begin{align*} \int \sin^5(2\theta)\cos(2\theta) \, d\theta \amp = \int \frac{1}{2} u^5 \, du\\ \amp = \frac{1}{2} \frac{u^6}{6} + C\\ \amp = \frac{\sin^6(2\theta)}{12} + C \end{align*}
