Use integration by parts with

\begin{align*} u \amp = x \amp dv \amp = e^{2x} \, dx\\ du \amp = 1 \, dx \amp v \amp = \dfrac{1}{2}e^{2x} \end{align*}

Then

\begin{align*} \int xe^{2x} \, dx \amp = x \cdot \frac{1}{2}e^{2x} - \int \frac{1}{2}e^{2x} \, dx\\ \amp = \frac{1}{2}xe^{2x} - \frac{1}{2} \cdot \frac{1}{2}e^{2x} + C \end{align*}
