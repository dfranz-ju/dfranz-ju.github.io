Use substitution with \(u = \sin(5\theta)\) and \(du = 5\cos(5\theta) \, d\theta\text{,}\) so \(\dfrac{1}{5} \, du = \cos(5\theta) \, d\theta\text{:}\)

\begin{align*} \int \sin(5\theta)\cos(5\theta) \, d\theta \amp = \int \frac{1}{5} u \, du\\ \amp = \frac{1}{5} \frac{u^2}{2} + C\\ \amp = \frac{\sin^2(5\theta)}{10} + C \end{align*}
