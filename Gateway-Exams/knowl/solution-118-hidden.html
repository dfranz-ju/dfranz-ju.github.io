Use integration by parts with

\begin{align*} u \amp = \ln(4x) \amp dv \amp = x \, dx\\ du \amp = \dfrac{1}{x} \, dx \amp v \amp = \dfrac{x^2}{2} \end{align*}

Then

\begin{align*} \int x\ln(4x) \, dx \amp = \ln(4x) \cdot \frac{x^2}{2} - \int \frac{x^2}{2} \cdot \frac{1}{x} \, dx\\ \amp = \frac{1}{2} x^2\ln(4x) - \int \frac{1}{2}x \, dx\\ \amp = \frac{1}{2} x^2 \ln(4x) - \frac{1}{2} \cdot \frac{x^2}{2} + C \end{align*}
