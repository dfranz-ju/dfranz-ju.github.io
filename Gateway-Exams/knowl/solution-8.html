Solution 1.1.8.1.

Solution 1: Rewrite and use the chain rule.

\begin{align*} h'(x) \amp = \frac{d}{dx}[(1+x^2)^{-1}]\\ \amp = -(1+x^2)^{-2} \cdot \frac{d}{dx}[1+x^2]\\ \amp = -(1+x^2)^{-2}\cdot 2x \end{align*}

Solution 2: Use the quotient rule.

\begin{align*} h'(x) \amp = \frac{(1+x^2) \cdot \frac{d}{dx}[1] - 1 \cdot \frac{d}{dx}[1+x^2]}{(1+x^2)^2}\\ \amp = \frac{(1+x^2)\cdot 0 - 1 \cdot 2x}{(1+x^2)^2}\\ \amp = \frac{-2x}{(1+x^2)^2} \end{align*}
