Solution 2.5.6.1.

Use integration by parts with

\begin{align*} u \amp = x \amp dv \amp = \sin(4x) \, dx\\ du \amp = 1 \, dx \amp v \amp = -\frac{1}{4}\cos(4x) \end{align*}

Then

\begin{align*} \int x\sin(4x) \, dx \amp = x\cdot -\frac{1}{4}\cos(4x) - \int - \frac{1}{4} \cos(4x) \, dx\\ \amp = -\frac{1}{4}x\cos(4x) + \frac{1}{4} \int \cos(4x) \, dx\\ \amp = -\frac{1}{4}x\cos(4x) + \frac{1}{4} \cdot \frac{1}{4}\sin(4x) + C \end{align*}
